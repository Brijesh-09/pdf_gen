Regarding Women or Men with Several Last Names, i.e.: Birth, Marriage, Remarried, etc.:

 

Give all name variations to the following documents:

1). Act of Expatriation and Oath of Allegiance (One Name per document)

2). Certificate of Assumed Name-Notice of Transfer of Reserved Name

3). Acknowledgment, Acceptance and Deed of Re-Conveyance / Conveyance

 

Regarding Notary Instructions: The notary in question is to fill out “Notary Section” of paperwork in Blue Ink Only.

 

Regarding Recording Secretary Instructions: The Recorder in question is to fill out “Recorder Section” of paperwork in Blue Ink Only. This includes but not limited too Recording Details atop of paperwork.

 

Regarding the 1779 Declaration document. Remember to sign this document by printing your name (John Ray Doe, Mary Jane Doe) on the signature line. Do not use cursive. Finally, it is recommended that this document be sealed by a notary with a “raised” seal (embossed) rather than the usual ink stamp. NOTE: Usual ink stamp is still valid if you are unable to locate notary with “raised” seal.

 

Regarding the Declaration of Political Status. This document should be sealed with a raised seal as well. NOTE: Usual ink stamp is still valid if you are unable to locate notary with “raised” seal.

 

Regarding the two Witness documents: The witnesses can be family, relatives, friends, etc., as long as the witness has known you for at least seven (7) years and is twenty-one (21) years of age. Witness Should Sign this document in blue, cursive without the red right thumb print.

 

Regarding your Autograph: When you sign these documents, please make sure you autograph them properly with your first, middle and last name respectively. NO ABBREVITION! The autograph should be legible as well. Please add a “C“ with round circle around it at end of your name to indicate Copyright. ©  (Please see example on first page.)

 

Regarding photo’s: These must be of head and shoulders on a plain white background with no shadows. No cell phone pics (Selfies). No glasses. These photos are the same as a passport photo. Note: Your picture must be a .jpg file and then dragged into the space provided on the witness form. If you do not have the means to acquire the .jpg, you can always go to a local pharmacy; Walgreens, Rite Aid, etc., and obtain a passport quality picture and have them saved to a thumb drive, and then downloaded onto your computer… Please note also, that you will need to have ready a dual set of .jpg mug shot/thumb print for your future ID as well.

 

Regarding your thumbprint: The thumbprint, in red ink, should touch the last few letters of the last name and be legible. When seeing the notary, both the signature and the thumbprint should be observed by the notary each time. Witness documents do not get a thumb print.

 

Regarding your birth certificate: You will need a birth certificate from your State Vital Statistics Office (most everybody gets three), a color “copy” of one will be sent to the Recording Secretary to be kept on file along with the other completed documents.
